   Ceruloplasmin (or caeruloplasmin) is a ferroxidase enzyme that in
   humans is encoded by the CP gene.
   Ceruloplasmin is the major copper-carrying protein in the blood, and in
   addition plays a role in iron metabolism. It was first described in
   1948. Another protein, hephaestin, is noted for its homology to
   ceruloplasmin, and also participates in iron and probably copper
   metabolism.
