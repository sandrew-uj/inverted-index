
   Russell Doughten and Donald W. Thompson, two Iowa-based filmmakers,
   formed Mark IV Pictures in 1972 to produce A Thief in the
   Night.:577-578 Thompson had been working in radio.:69
   Doughten had worked with Good News Productions on The Blob in 1958,
   and had produced other films in Iowa through his production company
   Heartland Productions.:7-8

   The film was produced in 1972 for a budget of $68,000. It earned
   roughly $4.2 million during its first decade of release, the majority
   of which came from audience donations. It was "one of the first films
   to take on Fundamentalist apocalyptic narratives within a fictional
   motif.":116
