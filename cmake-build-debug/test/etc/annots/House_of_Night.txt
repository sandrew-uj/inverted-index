   Zoey and her friends also face the usual teenage issues. Zoey keeps
   some secrets from her friends and gets in trouble with them, forcing
   her to consider the nature of friendship. Kristin Cast said that these
   moral dilemmas were included because "those are issues teenagers deal
   with... We're not afraid to discuss things that are actually
   happening."^[5] As with many contemporary vampire novels, exploration
   of human nature and social commentary forms a subtext.^[6] and Tsi
   Sgili^[7] are taken from real Cherokee legends.^[a]
